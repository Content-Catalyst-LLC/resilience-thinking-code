# Validation Plan

A professional food-and-water resilience workflow should include:

1. **Food availability validation**  
   Use production records, import/export data, reserves, storage capacity, crop diversity, livestock/fisheries data, logistics data, and emergency supply information.

2. **Water availability validation**  
   Use rainfall, river flow, reservoir storage, groundwater levels, recharge estimates, withdrawals, environmental flows, drought indicators, and seasonal variability.

3. **Access validation**  
   Assess affordability, distance, infrastructure access, service continuity, food insecurity, safe-water access, social protection, disability access, rights, and conflict conditions.

4. **Quality validation**  
   Review nutrition, food safety, spoilage, water quality, sanitation, salinity, pathogens, chemical contamination, and emergency testing systems.

5. **Ecosystem validation**  
   Assess soils, watersheds, aquifers, wetlands, biodiversity, pollination, environmental flows, erosion, recharge, and ecological thresholds.

6. **Infrastructure validation**  
   Review irrigation, pumping, treatment, storage, cold chains, roads, energy, digital monitoring, sanitation, and emergency distribution systems.

7. **Market and livelihood validation**  
   Review price volatility, household purchasing power, input markets, trade dependence, labor conditions, reserves, and smallholder viability.

8. **Equity and governance validation**  
   Assess disaggregated access, participation, land and water rights, Indigenous and local knowledge, allocation rules, recovery support, and institutional legitimacy.

9. **Learning review**  
   Track whether droughts, floods, contamination events, price shocks, and near misses revise policy, investment, monitoring, and community participation.
