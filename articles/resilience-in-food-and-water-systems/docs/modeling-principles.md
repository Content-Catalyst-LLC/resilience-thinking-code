# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold models food-and-water resilience as an integrated social-ecological, infrastructural, economic, and governance problem.

## Core variables

- availability
- access
- stability
- quality
- adaptive capacity
- equity protection
- resource-depletion risk
- climate stress
- market volatility
- infrastructure disruption/support
- ecosystem condition/stress
- resource coupling
- equity burden
- system performance under disturbance

## Core modeling logic

Food-and-water resilience value rises when availability, access, stability, quality, adaptive capacity, and equity protection increase. Resilience value falls when depletion risk, climate stress, market volatility, infrastructure disruption, ecosystem stress, and unequal burden increase.

The scaffold calculates:

1. food-water strategy value under multiple priority scenarios
2. equity-adjusted resilience value
3. food-water system performance under compound stress
4. depletion-risk diagnostics
5. access, quality, adaptation, and equity review triggers
6. Monte Carlo robustness under uncertainty
7. synthetic classifier outputs for high-value, low-depletion food-water strategies

## Interpretation

These examples are transparent modeling scaffolds, not empirical safety or resource-allocation claims. Real food-and-water resilience analysis requires hydrology, agronomy, water-quality testing, public-health evidence, household data, infrastructure records, market analysis, ecological monitoring, local and Indigenous knowledge, equity review, and accountable governance.
