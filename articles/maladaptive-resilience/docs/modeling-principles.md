# Modeling Principles

This scaffold models maladaptive resilience as harmful persistence plus lock-in, burden shifting, weak transformation, and insufficient harm reduction.

Persistence is not automatically good. A system may continue functioning while preserving exposure, inequality, ecological damage, extraction, institutional failure, or long-term fragility. Adaptive resilience increases when persistence is paired with harm reduction, lock-in reduction, equity, transformation capacity, ecological integrity, and low burden shifting.

The purpose of the scaffold is to make the difference between resilience worth strengthening and resilience worth transforming visible.
