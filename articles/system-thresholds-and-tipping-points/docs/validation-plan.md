# Validation Plan

A professional threshold or tipping-point model should include:

1. **System-boundary validation**  
   Define the system, essential function, regime state, scale, and time horizon.

2. **Threshold-evidence validation**  
   Identify whether thresholds are observed, inferred, modeled, hypothesized, or scenario-based.

3. **Pressure-variable validation**  
   Validate external pressures such as nutrient loading, heat, demand, disturbance, debt, trust erosion, or maintenance backlog.

4. **Feedback validation**  
   Identify feedback loops that could stabilize the current regime or amplify movement toward an alternative regime.

5. **Early-warning validation**  
   Test whether rolling variance, autocorrelation, recovery time, or spatial indicators are meaningful for the specific system.

6. **Hysteresis validation**  
   Evaluate whether the return path differs from the collapse path and whether recovery is plausible.

7. **Equity and governance review**  
   Assess who is closest to threshold, who created the risk, who has adaptive capacity, and who controls reorganization.

8. **Uncertainty review**  
   Label modeled, observed, inferred, and speculative parameters clearly.
