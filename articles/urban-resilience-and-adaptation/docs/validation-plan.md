# Validation Plan

A professional urban resilience workflow should include:

1. **Hazard validation**  
   Use heat, flood, coastal, wildfire smoke, drought, air-quality, and compound-event data.

2. **Exposure validation**  
   Map people, housing, critical services, infrastructure, public facilities, ecosystems, and economic functions at neighborhood or block scale.

3. **Vulnerability validation**  
   Use housing quality, rent burden, health conditions, age, disability, social isolation, legal status, service access, energy burden, pollution exposure, and historical disinvestment indicators.

4. **Service-continuity validation**  
   Review water, power, sanitation, transit, communications, cooling, food access, shelter, health care, waste, schools, and emergency response during outages or stress events.

5. **Ecological validation**  
   Assess tree canopy, wetlands, parks, soils, streams, green roofs, permeable surfaces, air-quality benefits, flood storage, heat reduction, biodiversity, and maintenance.

6. **Community validation**  
   Include local knowledge, trusted organizations, mutual aid capacity, language access, disability access, cultural context, and lived risk reports.

7. **Equity and anti-displacement validation**  
   Review who benefits, who pays, who is displaced, who participates, and who recovers last.

8. **Maladaptation validation**  
   Test risk transfer, false security, green displacement, emissions-intensive adaptation, digital exclusion, and relocation harms.

9. **Learning review**  
   Track whether disasters, heatwaves, floods, near misses, audits, and public feedback revise policy, budgets, standards, and implementation.
