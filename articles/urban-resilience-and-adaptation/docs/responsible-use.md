# Responsible Use

This scaffold is not a city safety certification, neighborhood ranking system, automated investment tool, or substitute for public planning.

Do not use it to:

- rank neighborhoods or communities without context, participation, and accountability
- justify displacement, disinvestment, policing, service withdrawal, or exclusion
- certify that urban infrastructure, housing, or public-health systems are safe
- replace engineering, public-health, housing, legal, ecological, or emergency management review
- hide unequal exposure, outage, recovery, or displacement behind a single resilience score
- treat synthetic data as empirical evidence
- ignore local knowledge, Indigenous rights, accessibility, language access, renters, informal workers, or unhoused residents

Use it to make assumptions visible, compare strategies transparently, identify uncertainty, support public learning, and strengthen accountable urban resilience planning.
