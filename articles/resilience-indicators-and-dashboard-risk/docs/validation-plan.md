# Validation Plan

A professional resilience indicator and dashboard workflow should include:

1. **System-definition validation**  
   Define resilience of what, to what, for whom, over what time horizon, and at what scale.

2. **Indicator validation**  
   Confirm that indicators represent meaningful exposure, sensitivity, resistance, recovery, adaptive capacity, threshold, and justice domains.

3. **Threshold validation**  
   Review whether warning levels and escalation rules reflect evidence, uncertainty, and governance responsibility.

4. **Data-quality validation**  
   Label missingness, update frequency, confidence, data source, bias, modeled estimates, and coverage gaps.

5. **Justice review**  
   Disaggregate exposure, recovery, service continuity, repair priority, access, and participation.

6. **Dashboard-risk review**  
   Test for false precision, metric fixation, hidden inequality, green-zone complacency, and weak decision triggers.

7. **Decision-link review**  
   Confirm what action follows when indicators worsen and who is accountable.

8. **Learning review**  
   Track whether dashboard warnings actually change rules, funding, design, maintenance, or public protection.
