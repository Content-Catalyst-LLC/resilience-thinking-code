# Validation Plan

A professional supply chain resilience workflow should validate redundancy, flexibility, visibility, coordination, adaptive capacity, equity safeguards, infrastructure continuity, systemic exposure, and implementation feasibility.

Important validation sources include multi-tier supplier data, procurement records, shipment records, inventory coverage, route dependency maps, logistics performance data, port and rail capacity, cyber-risk assessments, climate-risk maps, infrastructure-service continuity data, labor and human-rights due diligence, supplier finance data, emergency response records, and interviews with workers, suppliers, logistics operators, regulators, public agencies, and affected communities.

Quantitative indicators should be interpreted with confidentiality safeguards, public-interest review, labor and human-rights analysis, climate-risk context, critical-goods prioritization, and governance constraints.
