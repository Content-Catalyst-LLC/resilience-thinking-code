# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold models supply chain resilience as a redundancy, flexibility, visibility, coordination, adaptation, equity, infrastructure-continuity, systemic-exposure, and implementation problem.

## Core variables

- redundancy
- flexibility
- visibility
- coordination
- adaptive capacity
- equity safeguards
- infrastructure continuity
- systemic exposure
- implementation burden
- supplier concentration
- route chokepoint exposure
- inventory thinness
- climate exposure
- cyber dependency
- labor vulnerability
- infrastructure fragility
- coordination capacity
- flow performance under stress

## Core modeling logic

Supply chain resilience value rises when redundancy, flexibility, visibility, coordination, adaptive capacity, equity safeguards, and infrastructure continuity increase. Value falls when systemic exposure, implementation burden, supplier failure, logistics disruption, demand surge, inventory depletion, cyber disruption, climate pressure, labor disruption, coordination stress, and equity burden increase.

The scaffold calculates strategy value, equity-adjusted value, infrastructure-adjusted value, dynamic flow performance under repeated stress, implementation-burden diagnostics, visibility diagnostics, equity and labor safeguard diagnostics, and Monte Carlo robustness under uncertainty.
