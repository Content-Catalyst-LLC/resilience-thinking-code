# Modeling Principles

This scaffold models the difference between support-oriented resilience and abandonment risk.

Support-oriented resilience increases when protective effectiveness, material support, accessible recovery, governance inclusion, transformation potential, and exposure reduction are high. Abandonment risk increases when support gaps, recovery gaps, weak governance, repeated exposure, burden shifting, and weak public capacity persist.

The goal is not to replace public deliberation. The goal is to reveal when resilience language may be masking responsibility shift, support gaps, or normalized exposure.
