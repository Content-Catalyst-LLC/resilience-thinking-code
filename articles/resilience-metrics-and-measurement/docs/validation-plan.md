# Validation Plan

A professional resilience measurement framework should include:

1. **System-definition validation**  
   Specify resilience of what, to what, for whom, over what time horizon, and at what scale.

2. **Critical-function validation**  
   Define the functions that must continue under disturbance and the minimum acceptable performance threshold.

3. **Indicator validation**  
   Test whether indicators are causally connected to resilience rather than merely convenient to collect.

4. **Performance validation**  
   Compare indicator scores with observed or simulated disturbance performance where possible.

5. **Threshold validation**  
   Identify known thresholds, capacity limits, early warning signals, and uncertainty.

6. **Justice review**  
   Disaggregate exposure, backup capacity, service continuity, recovery burden, and repair investment.

7. **Data-quality review**  
   Label missing data, modeled estimates, subjective scores, data age, and measurement uncertainty.

8. **Decision-link review**  
   Define what actions follow when indicators worsen.
