# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold models social-ecological systems as coupled human-natural systems.

## SES variables

- ecological condition
- governance quality
- livelihood diversity
- infrastructure support
- knowledge integration
- social trust
- market pressure
- climate exposure
- adaptive capacity
- resource dependency

## Coupled dynamics

The scaffold simulates:

1. ecological regeneration
2. extraction pressure
3. social pressure
4. governance response
5. livelihood pressure
6. climate pressure
7. market shocks
8. SES resilience margin
9. threshold-risk flags

## Core interpretation

Social-ecological resilience is not only ecological recovery and not only social continuity. It emerges from interactions among ecological condition, governance, livelihoods, infrastructure, knowledge, trust, markets, climate, and adaptive capacity.

## Appropriate use

Use this scaffold for education, synthetic-data modeling, dashboard prototyping, article companion code, and professional methods design. Replace synthetic data with validated field evidence, institutional analysis, and affected-community review before real-world use.
