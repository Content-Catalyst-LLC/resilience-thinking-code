# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold models landscape resilience and disturbance regimes.

## Landscape-resilience variables

- spatial heterogeneity
- viable connectivity
- refugia capacity
- ecological memory
- disturbance pressure
- fragmentation
- governance capacity
- social vulnerability
- patch condition
- patch exposure
- buffer capacity
- recovery capacity
- connectivity weight
- resilience margin

## Core modeling logic

Landscape resilience depends on the spatial relationship among patches. Disturbance can be absorbed, redirected, amplified, or transmitted depending on heterogeneity, connectivity, refugia, ecological memory, buffers, governance capacity, and social exposure.

The scaffold calculates:

1. landscape resilience profiles
2. disturbance-risk indexes
3. patch-level resilience margins
4. disturbance-spread simulations
5. threshold-risk flags
6. scenario summaries
7. connectivity-risk and refugia diagnostics

## Appropriate use

Use this scaffold for education, synthetic-data modeling, dashboard prototyping, article companion code, and professional methods design. Replace synthetic data with validated GIS, remote-sensing, field, hydrological, ecological, hazard, and social vulnerability data before real-world use.
