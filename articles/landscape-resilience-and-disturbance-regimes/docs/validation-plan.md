# Validation Plan

A professional landscape-resilience model should include:

1. **Spatial boundary validation**  
   Define the landscape boundary, patch typology, ecological region, watershed, jurisdiction, and relevant scales.

2. **Land-cover validation**  
   Use remote sensing, field mapping, land-cover classification, and local knowledge to validate patch composition and configuration.

3. **Disturbance-regime validation**  
   Estimate frequency, intensity, severity, extent, timing, and interactions for fire, flood, drought, storms, pests, disease, heat, erosion, and human disturbance.

4. **Connectivity validation**  
   Distinguish beneficial connectivity from risk-transmission pathways. Validate species movement, hydrological connectivity, corridors, barriers, roads, and infrastructure pathways.

5. **Refugia and memory validation**  
   Identify surviving patches, seed sources, soil legacies, hydrological refugia, old-growth remnants, cold-air pockets, wetlands, and culturally managed refugia.

6. **Social vulnerability validation**  
   Integrate exposure, housing, mobility, health, income, infrastructure, public investment, insurance, and recovery capacity.

7. **Justice and governance review**  
   Determine who is exposed, who receives protection, who decides, whose knowledge counts, and whether risk is shifted across communities.
