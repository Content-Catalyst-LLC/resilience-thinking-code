# Responsible Use

This scaffold is not an automatic hazard-ranking or land-management decision system.

Do not use it to:

- rank communities or landscapes without validated evidence and participatory review
- claim precise fire, flood, or disturbance thresholds from synthetic data
- justify displacement, managed retreat, exclusion, or disinvestment without rights-based governance
- treat ecological restoration as a substitute for climate mitigation or infrastructure justice
- ignore Indigenous fire stewardship, local ecological knowledge, or community authority
- replace field ecology, hydrology, remote sensing, planning expertise, or public accountability

Use it to support transparent inquiry into how spatial pattern, disturbance regimes, refugia, ecological memory, connectivity, governance, and social vulnerability shape landscape resilience.
