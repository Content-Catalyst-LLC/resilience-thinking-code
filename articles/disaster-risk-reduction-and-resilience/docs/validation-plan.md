# Validation Plan

A professional DRR and resilience workflow should include:

1. **Hazard validation**  
   Use local hazard records, climate projections, hydrological data, fire risk maps, seismic maps, ecological evidence, and scenario testing.

2. **Exposure validation**  
   Map people, housing, infrastructure, ecosystems, livelihoods, cultural assets, critical facilities, and services exposed to hazard.

3. **Vulnerability validation**  
   Assess housing, health, income, age, disability, ecosystem condition, infrastructure quality, labor exposure, language access, and social protection.

4. **Capacity validation**  
   Review early warning, evacuation, emergency logistics, shelters, response staffing, public health capacity, mutual aid, recovery finance, and institutional coordination.

5. **Cascading-risk validation**  
   Map power, water, transport, health, communications, food, finance, housing, and governance dependencies.

6. **Maladaptation review**  
   Test whether DRR shifts risk, increases exposure, produces false security, displaces people, or locks systems into unsafe pathways.

7. **Justice review**  
   Assess who is exposed, who benefits, who pays, who decides, who recovers slowly, and whose knowledge counts.

8. **Learning review**  
   Track whether after-action reviews, near misses, community warnings, and dashboard indicators change funding, policy, maintenance, and planning.
