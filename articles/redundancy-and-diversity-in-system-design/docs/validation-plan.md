# Validation Plan

A professional redundancy and diversity model should include:

1. **Critical-function validation**  
   Define the functions that must continue under disturbance.

2. **Redundancy validation**  
   Confirm that backup pathways can actually preserve function under stress.

3. **Response-diversity validation**  
   Test whether overlapping pathways fail differently or share hidden exposure.

4. **Common-mode failure review**  
   Map shared dependencies such as suppliers, regions, fuels, platforms, workforce, legal authority, or assumptions.

5. **Maintenance validation**  
   Verify that backup systems are funded, tested, staffed, documented, and exercised.

6. **Coordination validation**  
   Confirm that diverse components are interoperable during stress.

7. **Justice review**  
   Assess who has access to redundancy, who lacks it, and whether backup capacity protects vulnerable groups.

8. **Uncertainty review**  
   Use scenario and Monte Carlo analysis to test whether rankings are robust under changing assumptions.
