# Resilience in Small Business and Local Economies

Companion repository folder for the article **Resilience in Small Business and Local Economies**.

This directory provides reproducible workflows for small business resilience strategy comparison, cash-runway simulation, local economic fragility diagnostics, equity-access analysis, workforce and owner-strain review, supplier and digital resilience assessment, community wealth analysis, local public-capacity review, and uncertainty-aware resilience planning.

## Correct article directory

```text
articles/resilience-in-small-business-and-local-economies/
```

## Purpose

This scaffold supports a professional small business and local economic resilience workflow:

1. Define local economic resilience strategies across liquidity support, workforce capacity, supply resilience, digital readiness, public capacity, community wealth, equity access, inequality risk, and implementation burden.
2. Compare strategies under balanced, liquidity-first, workforce-first, procurement-first, digital-first, community-wealth-first, equity-first, and implementation-aware priorities.
3. Simulate small business function under demand shocks, supplier delays, digital payment failures, extreme weather closures, rent and debt pressure, insurance disruption, and compound local economic stress.
4. Track business function, cash runway, customer demand, owner strain, fragility gap, adaptive capacity, public support, and equity-adjusted function over time.
5. Diagnose liquidity gaps, workforce gaps, supplier gaps, digital readiness gaps, public-capacity gaps, community-wealth gaps, equity-access gaps, inequality-risk triggers, and implementation burden.
6. Model uncertainty using Monte Carlo simulation.
7. Support responsible interpretation across small business resilience, local economic development, community wealth, CDFIs, public capacity, workforce resilience, procurement resilience, climate adaptation, and equitable recovery.
8. Provide multi-language examples in Python, R, Julia, SQL, Rust, Go, C, C++, and Fortran.
9. Export reproducible tables, figures, documentation, and notebook placeholders.

## Responsible-use note

The data are synthetic and the models are methodological examples. They are intended for learning, reproducible article companions, dashboard prototyping, local resilience strategy comparison, business support planning, and professional workflow demonstration.

Real small business and local economic resilience analysis requires participatory review, business-owner input, worker input, lender data, public program data, infrastructure evidence, local procurement data, disaster-risk information, commercial rent data, insurance data, and context-specific judgment.

Do not use this scaffold to deny assistance, rank businesses punitively, replace local knowledge, justify disinvestment, normalize owner burnout, obscure worker precarity, or make automated lending, grant, procurement, or enforcement decisions.
