# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold models modularity and cascading failure in complex systems.

## Core variables

- dependency network nodes
- dependency links
- coupling strength
- modularity
- redundancy
- isolation capacity
- common-mode exposure
- coordination readiness
- justice sensitivity
- justice-weighted impact
- probability of large cascade

## Core modeling logic

Cascade risk rises when coupling strength, dependency concentration, common-mode exposure, and critical-node centrality increase. Cascade risk falls when redundancy, isolation capacity, modular boundaries, coordination readiness, and effective governance are stronger.

The scaffold calculates:

1. cascade propagation after initial node failure
2. probability of large cascade
3. justice-weighted impact
4. strategy containment value under multiple priorities
5. Monte Carlo robustness under uncertainty
6. synthetic classifier outputs for high-containment, low-common-mode-risk strategies

## Interpretation

These examples are transparent modeling scaffolds, not safety claims. Real cascade-risk analysis requires empirical dependency mapping, domain expertise, engineering review, ecological knowledge, public-health and governance context, local knowledge, and accountable decision-making.
