# Validation Plan

A professional regime-shift or early-warning model should include:

1. **Regime-definition validation**  
   Define the current regime, possible alternative regime, essential function, and transition concern.

2. **Slow-variable validation**  
   Identify slow pressures that may move the system toward threshold proximity.

3. **Feedback validation**  
   Determine which feedbacks maintain the current regime and which could stabilize an alternative regime.

4. **Early-warning validation**  
   Test whether variance, autocorrelation, recovery time, spatial pattern, or near-miss indicators are meaningful for the specific system.

5. **Noise and data-quality review**  
   Evaluate missing data, measurement frequency, sensor changes, reporting bias, and external shocks.

6. **Scenario validation**  
   Compare gradual pressure, fast pressure, noisy monitoring, and adaptive-intervention scenarios.

7. **Justice review**  
   Assess who sees warning first, whose warnings are ignored, and who bears the cost of crossing.

8. **Uncertainty review**  
   Label modeled, observed, inferred, and speculative parameters clearly.
