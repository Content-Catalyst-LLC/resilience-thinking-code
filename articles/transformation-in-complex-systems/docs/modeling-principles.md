# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold models transformation pathway analysis in complex systems.

## Core variables

- adaptive support
- transformability
- governance readiness
- justice contribution
- ecological viability
- legitimacy
- resource feasibility
- structural risk
- adaptive limits
- cumulative stress
- transformative capacity
- pathway robustness

## Core modeling logic

Transformation becomes relevant when adaptation within the old regime is no longer sufficient to preserve function, legitimacy, ecological viability, or justice. Pathway scoring helps compare possible transformation strategies under different priorities, but scores are decision-support artifacts, not decisions.

The scaffold calculates:

1. transformation pathway rankings under multiple priority scenarios
2. transformation readiness diagnostics
3. structural-risk penalties
4. pathway robustness under uncertainty
5. Monte Carlo probability of ranking first or in the top two
6. synthetic transformation success-risk classifier outputs

## Interpretation

These examples are transparent modeling scaffolds, not empirical claims. Real transformation work requires affected-community authority, public deliberation, domain expertise, system history, uncertainty review, legal safeguards, and accountable governance.
