# Modeling Principles

This scaffold models ethical resilience as a justice-sensitive strategy comparison problem.

Strategies are evaluated across protective effectiveness, equity, governance legitimacy, recognition, accountability, burden shifting, and implementation burden. The goal is not to replace deliberation. The goal is to make value assumptions visible so they can be discussed, revised, and validated.

A strategy can score highly on protection while scoring poorly on justice. A strategy can reduce aggregate risk while shifting burdens onto vulnerable groups. Ethical resilience analysis should reveal these trade-offs rather than hide them.
