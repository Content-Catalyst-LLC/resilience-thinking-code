# Validation Plan

A professional infrastructure resilience workflow should include:

1. **Asset validation**  
   Use inspection records, maintenance history, condition ratings, design standards, age, capacity, and failure records.

2. **Service validation**  
   Define minimum service levels for power, water, transport, communications, health support, sanitation, logistics, and public safety.

3. **Dependency validation**  
   Map power-water-transport-communications-health-logistics dependencies, shared suppliers, digital control layers, and emergency access routes.

4. **Hazard and stress validation**  
   Use climate scenarios, flood maps, heat thresholds, wildfire risk, seismic risk, cyber threat models, supply-chain stress, and chronic maintenance backlog.

5. **Recovery validation**  
   Review repair crews, spare parts, mutual aid, procurement speed, access routes, backup systems, and restoration sequencing.

6. **Equity validation**  
   Assess differential outage duration, service quality, restoration order, affordability, accessibility, disability inclusion, medical dependence, and community participation.

7. **Cascade validation**  
   Stress test compound events and cross-network failures such as power-water-health, transport-logistics-health, cyber-control-service, and communications-coordination cascades.

8. **Learning review**  
   Track whether outages, near misses, exercises, and public feedback change standards, investment, maintenance, and governance.
