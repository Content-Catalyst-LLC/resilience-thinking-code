# Validation Plan

A professional slow-variable model should include:

1. **System-boundary validation**  
   Define system function, scale, geography, time horizon, affected groups, and relevant nested systems.

2. **Slow-variable validation**  
   Confirm which variables change slowly enough to shape future resilience and whether they can be measured reliably.

3. **Historical validation**  
   Compare modeled slow-variable trends to observed history, field evidence, maintenance records, monitoring data, or institutional archives.

4. **Threshold-distance validation**  
   Treat threshold distance as a scenario estimate unless calibrated to empirical evidence.

5. **Shock-interaction validation**  
   Test how fast shocks interact with slow conditions rather than treating shocks as isolated events.

6. **Monitoring validation**  
   Evaluate whether indicators are timely, disaggregated, trusted, and connected to authority.

7. **Justice review**  
   Assess who experiences slow harm first, whose signals are ignored, and who has capacity to respond.

8. **Uncertainty review**  
   Label modeled, observed, inferred, and speculative parameters clearly.
