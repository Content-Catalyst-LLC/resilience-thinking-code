# Resilience Modeling Principles

## 1. Specify the system
Resilience is always resilience of something.

## 2. Specify the disturbance
A system may be resilient to one disturbance and fragile to another.

## 3. Specify the function
Resilience concerns preserving or reorganizing essential functions.

## 4. Distinguish recovery, adaptation, and transformation
Returning to a prior state is not always possible or desirable.

## 5. Include threshold distance
Nonlinear change and regime shifts are central to resilience thinking.

## 6. Include equity and vulnerability
Resilience analysis must ask who is protected and who bears risk.

## 7. Avoid resilience-as-abandonment
Do not use resilience language to normalize underinvestment or preventable harm.
