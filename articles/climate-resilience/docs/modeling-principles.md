# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold models climate resilience as a multidimensional systems capacity.

## Core variables

- exposure reduction
- vulnerability reduction
- adaptive capacity
- recovery capacity
- transformative capacity
- justice protection
- maladaptation risk
- climate stress intensity
- slow-onset stress
- threshold proximity
- justice sensitivity
- system function under disturbance

## Core modeling logic

Climate resilience rises when exposure and vulnerability fall, adaptive and recovery capacity increase, transformation pathways are available where needed, and justice protections are strong. Climate resilience falls when maladaptation risk, threshold proximity, chronic stress, compound risk, and unequal burden increase.

The scaffold calculates:

1. climate resilience strategy value under multiple priorities
2. justice-weighted resilience value
3. system function under repeated climate shocks and slow stress
4. transformation and maladaptation diagnostics
5. Monte Carlo robustness under uncertainty
6. synthetic classifier outputs for high-value, low-maladaptation strategies

## Interpretation

These examples are transparent modeling scaffolds, not empirical claims. Real climate resilience analysis requires climate projections, local hazard evidence, infrastructure data, ecological monitoring, public health data, community participation, equity review, and accountable governance.
