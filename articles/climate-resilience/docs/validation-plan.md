# Validation Plan

A professional climate resilience workflow should include:

1. **Hazard validation**  
   Use local and regional climate projections, observed hazard records, slow-onset trends, and compound-risk scenarios.

2. **Exposure validation**  
   Map people, ecosystems, infrastructure, critical services, cultural assets, and livelihoods exposed to climate stress.

3. **Vulnerability validation**  
   Evaluate housing, health, income, water, labor, ecological, governance, and service-dependency conditions.

4. **Adaptive capacity validation**  
   Review monitoring, learning, governance flexibility, funding, local knowledge, institutional memory, and decision authority.

5. **Recovery validation**  
   Track recovery time, completeness, repair access, social protection, recovery funding, and uneven recovery by group and place.

6. **Transformation validation**  
   Identify where incremental adaptation becomes insufficient and evaluate relocation, land-use reform, ecosystem restoration, or livelihood transition pathways.

7. **Maladaptation review**  
   Test whether adaptation shifts risk, locks in unsafe development, increases emissions, displaces people, or deepens future vulnerability.

8. **Justice review**  
   Assess who is exposed, who benefits, who pays, who decides, who recovers slowly, and whose knowledge counts.
