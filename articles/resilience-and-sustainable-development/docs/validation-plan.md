# Validation Plan

A professional sustainable resilience workflow should validate ecological integrity, social inclusion, adaptive capacity, governance capacity, infrastructure continuity, economic resilience, and resource-pressure assumptions.

Important validation sources include greenhouse-gas inventories, biodiversity indicators, water stress data, land-use data, soil and watershed indicators, social vulnerability indices, public-health metrics, housing and energy-burden data, local economic indicators, infrastructure exposure data, institutional-capacity review, public finance data, community knowledge, Indigenous and local stewardship evidence, and participatory scenario planning.

Quantitative indicators should be interpreted with ecological science, historical injustice, public accountability, community participation, and uncertainty-aware decision-making.
