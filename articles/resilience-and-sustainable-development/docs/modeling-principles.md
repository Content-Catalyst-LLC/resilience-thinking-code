# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold models sustainable resilience as a viability problem across resilience, ecological integrity, social inclusion, economic sufficiency, governance capacity, adaptive capacity, resource pressure, and implementation burden.

## Core variables

- resilience
- ecological integrity
- social inclusion
- economic sufficiency
- governance capacity
- adaptive capacity
- resource pressure
- implementation burden
- climate exposure
- ecological stress
- social vulnerability
- economic fragility
- infrastructure exposure
- governance constraint
- equity sensitivity
- ecological overshoot
- dynamic development quality
- boundary-adjusted viability

## Core modeling logic

Sustainable resilience value rises when resilience, ecological integrity, social inclusion, economic sufficiency, governance capacity, and adaptive capacity increase. Viability value falls when resource pressure, ecological overshoot, implementation burden, climate stress, social vulnerability, economic fragility, infrastructure disruption, and governance stress increase.

The scaffold calculates pathway value, equity-adjusted viability, boundary-adjusted viability, dynamic development quality under repeated stress, implementation-burden diagnostics, resource-pressure diagnostics, and Monte Carlo robustness under uncertainty.
