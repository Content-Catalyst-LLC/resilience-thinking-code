# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold models adaptive capacity in complex systems.

## Adaptive-capacity variables

- learning
- flexibility
- diversity
- governance capacity
- slack
- trust and legitimacy
- rigidity and lock-in
- exposure
- disturbance load
- shock intensity
- system viability
- threshold risk

## Core modeling logic

Adaptive capacity is treated as response space. A system can maintain viability under disturbance when learning, flexibility, diversity, governance, slack, and trust offset exposure, disturbance, and rigidity.

The scaffold calculates:

1. adaptive-capacity profiles
2. adaptive-vulnerability indexes
3. response-space diagnostics
4. repeated-disturbance viability simulations
5. threshold-risk flags
6. scenario summaries
7. synthetic threshold-risk classifier outputs

## Interpretation

These examples are not empirical claims. They are transparent modeling scaffolds for thinking about how response space can be created, depleted, restored, and tested under uncertainty.
