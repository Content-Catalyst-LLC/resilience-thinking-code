# Validation Plan

A professional adaptive-capacity model should include:

1. **System definition validation**  
   Define system boundaries, essential functions, exposed groups, decision scales, and time horizon.

2. **Indicator validation**  
   Validate learning, flexibility, diversity, governance, slack, trust, rigidity, and exposure indicators with domain evidence.

3. **Disturbance validation**  
   Estimate disturbance loads, shock sequences, compound stressors, and uncertainty ranges.

4. **Viability validation**  
   Define what counts as critical-function loss or threshold risk for the system being studied.

5. **Governance validation**  
   Review whether institutions can act on feedback, revise rules, coordinate across boundaries, and remain legitimate.

6. **Equity review**  
   Assess whether adaptive capacity is broadly shared or concentrated among those with more wealth, power, mobility, or protection.

7. **Uncertainty review**  
   Label modeled, observed, inferred, and uncertain parameters clearly.
