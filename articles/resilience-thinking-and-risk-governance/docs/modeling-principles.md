# Modeling Principles

This companion scaffold connects risk governance and resilience thinking.

## Risk-governance variables

- hazard intensity
- exposure
- vulnerability
- trust
- participation quality
- knowledge integration
- coordination quality
- transparency
- accountability

## Resilience variables

- buffer capacity
- adaptive capacity
- learning capacity
- resilience margin
- threshold-risk state
- disturbance load
- governance stress

## Core modeling logic

Risk pressure is modeled as a function of hazard, exposure, vulnerability, and adaptive capacity.

Governance capacity is modeled as a composite of trust, participation, knowledge integration, coordination, transparency, and accountability.

Resilience margin is modeled as the remaining capacity after risk pressure, disturbance load, and vulnerability are compared against buffers, adaptive capacity, learning capacity, and governance capacity.

## Appropriate use

Use this scaffold for education, synthetic-data modeling, dashboard prototyping, article companion code, and professional methods design. Replace synthetic data with validated evidence before real-world use.
