# Modeling Principles

This companion folder models the history of resilience theory as an expanding conceptual field.

## What is being modeled?

The workflows represent historical phases as structured observations with stylized scores for:

- conceptual scope
- governance relevance
- system complexity
- justice relevance

These scores are not claims of historical measurement. They are transparent modeling assumptions used to demonstrate how intellectual history can be represented in reproducible analytical form.

## Key distinction

The scaffold separates:

- **Historical narrative**: the article’s interpretive account.
- **Knowledge modeling**: structured representation of periods, concepts, and sources.
- **Dynamical modeling**: simple mathematical examples that show why resilience theory moved beyond equilibrium-return logic.

## Appropriate use

Use this scaffold for teaching, documentation, reproducible article companions, knowledge mapping, and conceptual analysis. Do not use the synthetic scores as bibliometric evidence without replacing them with real source data and a documented methodology.
