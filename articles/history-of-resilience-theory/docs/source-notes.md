# Source Notes

A rigorous historical analysis of resilience theory should engage:

- C.S. Holling's 1973 distinction between resilience and stability.
- Gunderson and Holling's adaptive cycle and panarchy work.
- Walker, Holling, Carpenter, and Kinzig on resilience, adaptability, and transformability.
- Folke's synthesis of social-ecological resilience.
- Disaster risk reduction and climate-adaptation definitions.
- Critical scholarship on resilience, vulnerability, power, and burden shifting.

This scaffold does not automate historical interpretation. It provides a reproducible structure for organizing concepts, periods, source metadata, and illustrative models.
