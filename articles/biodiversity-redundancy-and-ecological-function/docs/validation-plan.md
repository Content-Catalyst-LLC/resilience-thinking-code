# Validation Plan

A professional biodiversity and ecological-function model should include:

1. **Taxonomic validation**  
   Confirm species presence, abundance, and community composition with defensible ecological data.

2. **Trait validation**  
   Validate trait assignments, functional groups, and trait-function relationships.

3. **Redundancy validation**  
   Determine whether species that appear functionally redundant truly perform comparable roles across contexts.

4. **Response-diversity validation**  
   Estimate variation in sensitivity to drought, heat, fire, flood, disease, pesticides, habitat loss, and other disturbances.

5. **Connectivity validation**  
   Assess landscape/waterscape connectivity, dispersal pathways, gene flow, habitat corridors, and refugia.

6. **Function validation**  
   Compare modeled functional output with observed ecosystem processes such as pollination, decomposition, nutrient cycling, regeneration, or water filtration.

7. **Justice and governance review**  
   Assess rights, local stewardship, Indigenous knowledge, conservation burdens, and access to biodiversity benefits.
