# Ethical Use of Resilience Thinking

Resilience thinking should not be used to normalize preventable harm, shift burden onto vulnerable communities, or preserve unjust systems.

## Guardrails

- Ask what is being made resilient.
- Ask who benefits from continuity.
- Ask who bears disturbance and adaptation burden.
- Distinguish resilience from abandonment.
- Distinguish recovery from justice.
- Identify maladaptive resilience.
- Preserve local knowledge and participation.
- Keep models contestable and revisable.
